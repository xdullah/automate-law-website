01 — THE PROBLEM

Your next case is sitting in a form submission from last Tuesday at 9:42pm.

A claimant searches at 9pm. They fill out three forms. The firm that calls back first signs them. Most PI firms don't call back until 9am the next morning—if at all. By then, the case is already on a contingency agreement somewhere else.

This isn't a marketing problem. It's an intake problem. You can buy more leads, run more ads, hire more paralegals—and still bleed 30–50% of qualified intakes to the firm that responds faster. Manual intake doesn't scale, and SaaS chatbots feel like SaaS chatbots.

02 — THE MECHANISM

Hardware in your office. Local LLM. Workflow enforcement on top.

PI ENGINE isn't a chatbot widget or a Zapier flow. It's a dedicated NVIDIA Jetson appliance we install on your firm's network, running a custom-trained language model that understands your intake criteria, your tone, and your case types. Every channel routes through it. Every interaction is logged, qualified, and enforced against your follow-up sequence.

STAGE 01 // CAPTURE

Every channel, one pipeline.

Web forms, paid ads, CallRail tracking, SMS short codes, chat. Inbound signal hits PI ENGINE within seconds of submission—including after-hours, weekends, and holidays.

STAGE 02 // RESPOND

Sub-60-second qualification.

The local LLM places a voice call, SMS, or email in your firm's voice. It qualifies the claim against your criteria—statute, jurisdiction, fault, severity, prior representation—and books a screened intake call.

STAGE 03 // ENFORCE

Nothing falls through.

PI ENGINE owns the follow-up sequence. If a lead doesn't pick up, it persists. If an attorney misses the callback, it escalates. Daily reports surface what closed, what stalled, and where revenue leaked.

04 — WHY LOCAL MATTERS

Cloud SaaS is the wrong shape for a PI firm.

Every cloud-based legal AI tool ships your client conversations to a third-party server. That's a privilege problem, a vendor lock-in problem, and a long-term unit-economics problem. PI ENGINE solves all three by running on your network, on hardware you own.

Cloud SaaS Intake Tool

  • Client conversations transit a third-party API
  • Per-seat or per-conversation pricing — costs scale with growth
  • You don't own the model or the data
  • Outages take you offline mid-intake
  • Vendor can change pricing, sunset features, or get acquired
  • Generic prompts trained on everyone's firm

PI ENGINE

  • All inference happens on hardware in your office
  • Flat retainer — costs don't scale with case volume
  • You own the appliance after deployment
  • Functions during internet outages for local intake
  • Configuration versioned in your firm's repository
  • Custom-trained on your firm's voice and criteria
